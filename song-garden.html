Song Garden Music Time

About

Nina Lane and Bryan Owens present Song Garden Music Time

Many young children have an innate love of music and movement. Song Garden Music Time is an interactive, playful program that celebrates the joy of music, movement, and song in a nurturing group setting for children aged approximately 2-8 years. Sessions feature a children's repertoire of classic and contemporary songs, accompanied live on acoustic guitar, mandolin, and fiddle.


Nina Lane and Bryan Owens began their musical collaboration in 1998 and bring decades of experience as professional musicians, songwriters, recording artists, and educators. After touring extensively throughout New Zealand, they relocated to Portland, Oregon, USA in 2001, where they worked in the music and education fields for over 20 years. In 2009, they co-founded a Steiner-inspired program, Song Garden School, and facilitated weekly children's music programs for 11 years.


Bryan Owens received his Certificate of Waldorf School Educators in 2004 and has taught in both early childhood and primary grades. His teaching experience includes Early Head Start, Portland Waldorf School, Swallowtail, Shining Star, and Song Garden School in the USA. Bryan has a particular passion for traditional folk music, specialising in tunes from around the world.


Nina Lane studied at the Micha-el Early Childhood Teachers Institute in Portland, Oregon. Her training included Simplicity Parenting, Boys Alive!, Juniper Tree School of Puppetry and Arts, Positive Discipline, and the Steiner approach to music and the young child. From 2000-2019, she worked as an early childhood educator at Early Head Start, New Day School, Cedarwood Waldorf School and Song Garden School. Nina also brings a deep passion for creative songwriting.


After concluding a 20-year chapter in the USA, Nina and Bryan have returned to New Zealand and now call Northland home. Nina teaches singing one-on-one with adults and children and facilitates a creative songwriting group in Whangārei. Bryan works as a music educator in traditional folk music and with Whangārei Youth Music.
